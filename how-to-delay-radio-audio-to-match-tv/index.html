How to delay radio audio to match TV

Radio commentary is often ahead of the TV picture during live sports. This guide compares the practical ways to delay the audio and bring both signals back into sync.

Why radio and TV fall out of sync

When you watch a match on TV and listen to radio commentary, the radio may describe a goal, a penalty or a key play several seconds before it appears on screen.

This is usually caused by latency in the television path: video encoding, buffering, distribution, smart TV apps, set-top boxes and streaming platforms can all add delay. The result is simple: the audio you prefer arrives before the picture you are watching.

The solution is not to make the TV faster. The practical solution is to delay the radio audio until it lines up with the image.

The main ways to delay radio commentary

1. Use an iPhone delay app

If the station provides a compatible online stream URL, a dedicated app can play that stream and delay it directly on the iPhone. Soccer RadioTv Sync was built specifically for this: add the stream, tap Sync when you hear a recognizable moment, tap again when you see it on TV, then fine-tune with + and –.

2. Use a radio receiver with delay

Dedicated delay radios can work with traditional radio signals, but they require extra hardware and tend to be much more expensive than a mobile app. They are also tied to the signals the receiver can tune.

3. Use desktop software such as VLC or audio-delay tools

Computer-based setups can delay audio, but they usually involve more configuration, audio routing, cables or keeping a laptop near the TV. They can be powerful, but they are not the most convenient solution during a live match.

4. Pause the TV or DVR

In some setups you can pause the TV picture until the radio catches up. This may work occasionally, but it is awkward and can be disrupted by ad breaks, app behavior or changes in the live stream.

Where Soccer RadioTv Sync fits

Soccer RadioTv Sync is designed for iPhone users who want a focused, low-resource way to synchronize online radio commentary with the TV picture.

  • Normally only 10–40 seconds are needed, but the app supports up to 300 seconds of adjustable delay.

Why the app exists

Soccer RadioTv Sync was born from a very specific frustration. Its creator, a lifelong football fan, wanted to keep watching matches on TV while listening to the radio commentator he had followed for more than 30 years.

When TV viewing shifted to streaming, the picture often arrived late and the radio spoiled key moments before they appeared on screen. A first private prototype was built on a 2013 MacBook. When friends heard about it, they thought it was a geeky idea, but also the kind of thing that would be great to carry on the iPhone. Searching forums and social networks then showed that it was not a personal oddity: the same frustration appeared in many countries and sports. The teams, broadcasters and languages changed, but the problem was the same.

Using the same synchronization core, that private prototype became the commercial iPhone version: Soccer RadioTv Sync.

FAQ

Why does radio commentary often arrive before the TV picture?

The main reason is not that radio waves are faster. It is usually the processing, encoding, buffering and distribution delay added by modern TV platforms, especially when the picture reaches you through streaming.

What is the easiest way to delay radio audio to match TV?

For an iPhone user listening to an online radio stream, the easiest method is a dedicated delay app such as Soccer RadioTv Sync. It delays the stream directly on the phone and lets you fine-tune the result during the match.

Can I use VLC or other desktop software instead?

Yes, desktop software can delay audio, but it normally requires a computer, more setup and sometimes audio routing. It can work, but it is less convenient during a live match.

Do I need a direct radio stream URL?

Yes. Soccer RadioTv Sync needs a compatible direct online radio stream URL, or a TXT, M3U or JSON list containing compatible stream URLs.

Does the app include radio stations?

The current version does not include a built-in station directory. Users add their own compatible stream URL or import a list. A simpler station discovery workflow is being developed.

How much delay is normally needed?

Normally only 10 to 40 seconds are needed, but Soccer RadioTv Sync supports up to 300 seconds when more range is required.
